About Me — Get to know this person!

Background

Hello there! I am a first-year BSIT–Multimedia Arts and Animation student at Universidad de Dagupan. I chose this path to focus on digital creation and storytelling beyond drawing.

I started drawing at a young age as a way to express myself, and over time shifted from traditional mediums to digital art using ibis Paint. I began taking art more seriously during the pandemic, and despite burnout at times, it continues to shape how I create today.

Goals

My goal is to create manga that connects with readers emotionally—stories that help them reflect, feel understood, or see themselves in characters and experiences.

I also want to explore 2D and 3D animation to express ideas in different ways and continue learning storytelling techniques. In the future, I hope to reach wider audience, collaborate with other creators, and continue improving my craft while creating work that truly resonates with people and leaves lasting impact.

Story

I used to see art as just a hobby until reading The Flowers of Evil by Shūzō Oshimi, which made me realize how powerful storytelling can be and how deeply it can affect people emotionally.

Since then, I’ve been drawn to emotional, often overlooked themes like internal struggles, mental health, and how the past shapes people and their decisions. Through my work, I want to create stories that help others feel seen, understood, and less alone in their experiences.
